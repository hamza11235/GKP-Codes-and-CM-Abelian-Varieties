# Phase 6: preregistered generic controls

Phase 6 compares every one of the 4,165 Phase-5 CM candidates with generic-real
controls generated before inspecting the outcomes. It is the first
population-wide *paired* CM-versus-generic experiment in this workstream.

## Frozen protocol

For each CM candidate and each of two deformation regimes, three controls were
generated by deterministic `rational * pi` symplectic deformations. Seeds are
the SHA-256 hash of the protocol version, stable candidate identifier, and
regime. There is no rejection, replacement, or adaptive resampling.

| regime | controls per CM candidate | generator amplitude | steps | vector bound |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| local | 3 | 0.002 | 4 | 2 |
| broad | 3 | 0.05 | 4 | 1 |

The complete experiment contains 24,990 controls. Every control preserves the
dimension, integral polarization matrix, polarization type, and metric
normalization of its paired CM baseline. A generic real deformation is non-CM
almost surely, but individual endomorphism rings are not certified.

The primary response is the paired change in relative systole,

$$
\Delta \ell^2 = \ell^2_{\mathrm{control}}-\ell^2_{\mathrm{CM}}.
$$

Thus a negative value favors the CM baseline. The analysis first averages the
three controls belonging to the same CM candidate; candidates, rather than
individual controls, are the independent units.

## Paired distance result

The local-regime mean is lower than its paired CM value for every tested type.
The intervals below are descriptive 95% normal intervals for the mean paired
difference across candidates.

| type | CM candidates | mean control minus CM ell^2 | 95% interval | mean control/CM ratio | fraction whose control mean beats CM |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| `(1,3)` | 876 | -0.026669 | [-0.029882, -0.023457] | 0.953813 | 0.393836 |
| `(1,5)` | 915 | -0.009892 | [-0.011767, -0.008017] | 0.980396 | 0.567213 |
| `(1,1,2)` | 1,051 | -0.005318 | [-0.006103, -0.004534] | 0.993520 | 0.392008 |
| `(1,1,3)` | 1,070 | -0.003865 | [-0.004529, -0.003201] | 0.994355 | 0.393458 |
| `(1,2,2)` | 253 | -0.005397 | [-0.006673, -0.004120] | 0.989711 | 0.245059 |

Pooling all 4,165 paired candidates in the local regime gives

$$
\mathrm{mean}(\Delta\ell^2)=-0.010445,
\qquad 95\%\ \mathrm{interval}=[-0.011322,-0.009568].
$$

The mean relative change is -1.77%. This aggregate is descriptive: the five
bounded candidate populations are not samples from a canonical distribution
on moduli space.

The broader regime also has a negative aggregate mean difference (-0.018601),
and four of the five type-wise means are negative. Type `(1,1,3)` is the
exception, with mean difference +0.002515 and an interval containing zero.
Broad-deformation conclusions are less clean because fixed generator
coefficients do not imply a uniformly bounded metric displacement on
ill-conditioned inputs.

## Passive-gate audit

Gate enumeration is much more expensive than the distance calculation. Before
outcomes were inspected, 25 candidates per type and regime were selected by a
second SHA-256 ranking, and control index zero was audited. This gives 250
outcome-independent selected controls.

An exhaustive numerical automorphism enumeration was found to have pathological
runtime on some deformed metrics before scientific outcomes were produced. A
fixed one-second cap was therefore recorded as a computational amendment; the
control samples and audit selection were not changed. Results are:

- 233 selected controls were exhaustively certified;
- 17 reached the fixed cap and remain `bounded_unresolved`;
- all 233 certified controls had exactly the generic minimal logical image;
- none had enhanced passive logical symmetry;
- 50 of the 233 paired CM candidates had a strictly larger logical image.

The last fraction is not expected to be one: Phase 5 showed that many CM points
also have only the minimal image. The result tests the sharper statement that
generic deformation destroys the extra passive symmetry when it is present.

## Important deformation caveat

`local` and `broad` refer to the fixed coefficients of the symplectic
generators, not to a fixed Frobenius distance between metrics. The median
relative metric displacement is 0.0835 in the local regime and 0.8033 in the
broad regime, but the distributions have long tails. In particular, the broad
95th percentile is about 84.97 because some baseline metrics are highly
ill-conditioned.

This does not invalidate the preregistered experiment; it reveals a limitation
of this control parameterization. A valuable follow-up is a second,
independently preregistered experiment using deformations normalized by actual
metric displacement or geodesic distance in the compatible-metric space.

## Claim boundary

The experiment supports:

> Under this deterministic paired-deformation protocol, the bounded CM
> candidates have a higher mean local relative systole in every tested
> polarization type, and every completed generic-control gate audit has only
> the minimal passive logical image.

It does not establish a canonical CM-versus-non-CM average, individual non-CM
certification, global optimality, or a theorem that CM forces either property.

Row-level results are in `data/phase6_generic_controls.json` and `.csv`;
aggregate results and the frozen protocol are stored in
`data/phase6_generic_controls_summary.json` and
`data/phase6_preregistered_protocol.json`. Ten extremal control distances were
independently recomputed at 60 decimal digits in
`data/phase6_high_precision_audit.json`.
